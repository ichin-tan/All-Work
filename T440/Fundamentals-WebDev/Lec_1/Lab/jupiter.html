Welcome to Jupiter

Climate

Jupiter is famed for its extreme weather conditions, with powerful storms and winds that can reach speeds of over 600 kilometers per hour. The Great Red Spot, a gigantic storm larger than Earth itself, has been swirling for centuries.

Wind Sailing Adventures

Experience the thrill of wind sailing on Jupiter's upper atmosphere, where the intense wind speeds can take you from the north pole to the south pole in just under an hour. This adrenaline-pumping activity is a must for adventure seekers!

Culture and Customs

Jupiter's floating colonies have a culture deeply rooted in science and exploration. The local customs include celebrating new astronomical discoveries and participating in weather prediction contests.

Cultural Attractions and Dining

Visit the Juno Hall, a museum dedicated to the history of space exploration on Jupiter. For dining, enjoy a meal at the Stratospheric Diner, which offers panoramic views of Jupiter's multicolored clouds.

Unique Attractions

Don't miss a guided tour of the Galilean Moons, accessible via short space shuttle trips. Each moon has its own unique environment and offers different activities, from ice skiing on Europa to volcano watching on Io.

Eco-Tourism

Participate in ecological preservation efforts by joining conservation projects aimed at protecting the delicate balance of Jupiter's upper atmosphere and its moons.
