Welcome to Mercury

Discover the Swiftness of Mercury: The Solar System’s Speedster!

Climate

Mercury’s extreme temperatures are a fascination for many. With no atmosphere to retain heat, temperatures can drop to -180°C at night and soar to 430°C during the day. Perfect for those who love experiencing the extremes of space weather!

Culture and Local Customs

While Mercury doesn’t host an indigenous population, the various research stations populated by scientists from across the galaxy have developed a unique subculture of their own. Expect a blend of scientific rigor with a passion for solar phenomena.

Cultural Attractions and Dining

Enjoy a visit to the Mercury Lounge, a dining hall that offers panoramic views of the Caloris Basin. The lounge serves a variety of dishes prepared by our robotic chefs, featuring ingredients sourced from across the solar system.

Unique Attractions

Mercury is home to the Great Valley, one of the largest fault systems in the Solar System. Stretching over 1,000 kilometers, it’s a sight not to be missed. Take a rover tour to explore this and other geological wonders.

Eco-Tourism

While traditional eco-tourism isn’t applicable, Mercury offers 'geo-tourism' which focuses on the preservation of its unique geological features. Programs include guided tours to lesser-known yet significant craters and terrain formations.
